Recommendations for students pursuing a degree in Computer Science or Data Science

General information

The key words "MUST", "MUST NOT", "REQUIRED", "SHALL", "SHALL NOT", "SHOULD", "SHOULD NOT", "RECOMMENDED", "MAY", and "OPTIONAL" in this document are to be interpreted as described in RFC2119.

Computer Science

Course map clarification

  • You SHOULD complete CS130, CS140, CS150, CS160, and Development Tools Proficiency (DT-PROFICIENCY) during the first year. The best time to complete DT-PROFICIENCY is J-Term or Summer.
  • You SHOULD complete DT-PROFICIENCY on KATIE prior to taking CS160.
  • You MUST complete DT-PROFICIENCY prior to registering for CS252, CS260, or CS330.
  • The combination of CS252 and CS260 is required for many 3xx and 4xx courses. this fact is imporatant if you are trying to squeeze your plan into 7, 6, or 5 semesters.
  • The combination of CS160 (Python, data structures), CS252 (Java, object-oriented programming), and CS330 (JavaScript, full stack development) SHOULD be sufficient while applying for internships.
  • You SHOULD take CS330 prior to starting the senior project.
  • You MUST take any 2 (two) 4xx courses (DS420 is another option).
  • While our curriculum is mostly "flat" and you can take courses in different orders, it takes 5 (five) semesters to complete the core sequence of CS150, CS160, CS260, CS490, and CS491.

See Academic Catalog for details and course descriptions.

Always consult with your academic adviser prior to registering for any class.

Data Science

Course map clarification

  • You SHOULD complete CS130, CS140, CS150, and DS120 prior to taking DS320.
  • All 3 subject area courses (SACs) SHOULD be from the same area. Talk to your advisor about your choice of the Subject Area.
